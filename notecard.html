MAT 107 cheat card

Use π = 3.14 (type 3.14 — don’t use the π key). Round only at the end.

Perimeter & area

  • Square P=4s · A=s²
  • Rectangle P=2L+2W · A=LW
  • Triangle P=a+b+c · A=½bh
  • Circle C=2πr · A=πr²

Volume

  • Cube V=s³
  • Cylinder V=πr²h
  • Sphere V=(4/3)πr³

Other

  • Pythagorean a²+b²=c²
  • Slope rise/run
  • Z-score z=(x−μ)/σ

Conversions

  • 1 yd = 3 ft
  • 1 sq yd = 9 sq ft
  • 1 cu yd = 27 cu ft
  • 1 gal = 3.785 L
  • 9 sq ft = 1 sq yd

Stats quick

  • mean = sum ÷ n
  • median = middle (sorted)
  • mode = most frequent
  • range = max − min
  • s ≈ range ÷ 4

Scaling

  • Area ∝ (scale)²
  • Volume ∝ (scale)³

Steps to solve

  1. Read — underline what is asked; list knowns.
  2. Choose — pick the matching formula / rule.
  3. Substitute — plug numbers (r = d/2 if needed).
  4. Compute — left→right; parentheses; π=3.14.
  5. Finish — convert units; round as asked.
Example A · Rectangle area 12 ft × 9 ft → A = L×W = 12×9 = 108 sq ft
Example B · Cylinder volume d=4 in, h=6 in → r=2 → V=3.14×2²×6 = 75.36 cu in
Example C · Z-score x=85, μ=70, σ=10 → z=(85−70)/10 = 1.50

Back · Normal curve

Empirical rule (≈normal data)

About 68% within 1 SD · 95% within 2 SD · 99.7% within 3 SD of the mean.

Below μ−1σ ≈ 16%. For exact z↔percentile values, use the table (not this sketch).

Worked examples — calculate these

1 · Within 1 SD
Ages ~ normal, mean 40, SD 5. About what % between 35 and 45?
35=μ−1σ, 45=μ+1σ → ≈ 68%
2 · Within 2 SD
Scores ~ normal, mean 80, SD 10. About what % between 60 and 100?
60=μ−2σ, 100=μ+2σ → ≈ 95%
3 · Upper tail beyond 3 SD
Mean 70, SD 5. About what % older than 85?
85=μ+3σ → each outer tail ≈ 0.15%
